Why Traditional Methods Are Failing Our Pets

For decades, we've relied on two main methods to identify lost pets: engraved tags and microchips. Both have their place, but both have critical limitations that leave our pets vulnerable.

Engraved tags wear down over time. The information becomes illegible. And they only work if someone can read them and wants to call you — there's no way to know your pet has been found until a stranger decides to reach out.

Microchips are better, but they require a trip to a vet's office with a specialized scanner. In the crucial first hours when your pet is most likely to be found, this creates a significant barrier. Most good samaritans don't have time to visit a vet — they need a way to help immediately.

"I've seen pets with microchips spend weeks in shelters because no one scanned them. The technology exists to identify them — it's just not accessible enough when it matters most."
